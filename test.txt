The 2008 financial crisis is characterized as a highly severe economic event, widely noted as the most serious financial crisis the world has faced since the Great Depression. The primary economic causes included a housing market bubble fueled by subprime mortgages and predatory lending. Key events included the collapse of Lehman Brothers in September 2008, which triggered a global panic. The global economic consequences were severe, leading to a worldwide recession, massive job losses, and millions of foreclosures. In response, governments implemented massive bailouts and enacted new regulatory responses like the Dodd-Frank Act to prevent future crises.
